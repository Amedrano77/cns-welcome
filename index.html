Workday onboarding

Your onboarding tasks, one card at a time.

These all live in Workday at login.tstc.edu. Each card opens to show exactly what to click. Closed by default so you only see one thing at a time — open the one you're working on.

  • Log in to Workday at login.tstc.edu with your TSTC username and password.
  • On your home page, find the "Awaiting Your Action" card and click "Go to My Tasks."
  • Or click the "My Tasks" inbox icon in the upper-left corner near your profile.
  • Open a task, click "E-sign by Adobe Sign," then Continue / Start to read through it.
  • At the signature block, type your name, click Apply, then Click to Sign and Submit.
💡
Each task has a short description at the top. Read it before you sign — you can always review the document first.
  • In My Tasks, open "Consent: Consent to Receive Form 1098-T Electronically."
  • Read the info — 1098-T reports your qualified tuition and may help with education tax credits.
  • Check the box: "I consent to receive my IRS Form 1098-T electronically."
  • Click Submit. You'll see a confirmation.
📅
Once consented, your form is available in Workday each year by January 31.
  • Open "Financial Aid State Aid Eligibility Form" and click E-sign by Adobe Sign → Continue.
  • Click Start, then answer each question (citizenship, Texas residency, etc.) and click Next.
  • Type your name and student ID in the boxes provided.
  • Click "Click to sign," type your full name, click Apply, and enter the date (MM/DD/YYYY).
  • Click Submit, then Done.
⚠️
You may need to complete this each academic year. Incomplete forms can delay your aid — questions go to the Financial Aid Office, 325-670-1400.
  • Open "Student Residency Event" and click Start Questionnaire.
  • Answer each question honestly (some students get extra sections based on answers).
  • Work through all sections until you reach the signature page.
  • Type your full name to sign, then Submit.
⚠️
Answers must be accurate and may need documentation. Inaccurate answers can cause reclassification and fees. The Registrar's Office reviews and notifies you by TSTC email.
  • Open "Academic Fresh Start Questionnaire" from your inbox and read the info.
  • Click Next, then answer Yes or No to whether you wish to establish an academic fresh start.
  • Click Submit, then Done.
ℹ️
Brand-new to TSTC? You won't see this task — skip it. It's only for students returning after an absence. Answering "No" never hurts your enrollment.
  • In My Tasks, open "TSIA2 Pre-Assessment" and click Start.
  • Pick a subject: Reading, Writing, or Math.
  • Answer the questions — it's not timed and there's no penalty for guessing.
  • Review your results; they show whether you're ready for the TSIA2 or could use some prep.
😌
This is not a pass/fail test and it doesn't affect your admission. It just points you to the right starting line.
  • Open "Consent: TSI Assessment Virtual" in My Tasks and click Next.
  • Read the virtual testing requirements (quiet space, photo ID, working webcam & mic).
  • Check the box to agree to the terms, then Submit.
  • You can now schedule your virtual TSI Assessment.
🎧
You consent each time you test virtually. Test in a quiet, private spot with a reliable connection and a government-issued photo ID. Testing Center: 325-670-1397.

    Gear up

    Supplies & books.

    Just the essentials to get going. One required item, a couple of optional kits, and your first-semester books are already covered.

    💻 Your laptop / desktop

    • 32 GB RAM recommended
    • CPU: 1 GHz+ 64-bit, 2+ cores (Intel 8th Gen+ / AMD Ryzen 2000+)
    • 128 GB USB drive or higher Required
    • USB-C Ethernet adapter (if your laptop has no Ethernet port)
    🚫 No Chromebooks, iPads, Surface Pros, or MacBooks.

    🧰 Handy tool kits

    • PC Repair Kit — Amazon ≈ $9 Optional
    • Networking Tool Kit — Amazon ≈ $27 Optional
    🛠️
    Neither kit is required — they're just nice to have if you want to tinker at home.

    📚 First-semester books — already covered

    ITNW 1308
    Implementing & Supporting Client Operating Systems
    ITNW 1358
    Network +
    ITSC 1325
    PC Hardware
    📖
    You'll have access to the Computer Networking books for these three courses. Syllabi open in Canvas Monday morning, the first day of the semester. General-ed books come through RedShelf (electronic) — you choose to accept or reject them. Prefer a hard copy? The course syllabi provides the ISBN, author, title & edition.
